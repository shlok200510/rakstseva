What is Plasma Donation?

Plasma is the liquid portion of your blood that contains essential proteins and antibodies. Unlike whole blood donation, plasma donation allows you to donate more frequently as your body replaces plasma within 24-48 hours.

During plasma donation, blood is drawn from your arm and passed through a machine that separates plasma from other blood components. The red blood cells and other components are then returned to your body.

Why Plasma Donation Matters

  • Treats patients with bleeding disorders
  • Helps burn and trauma victims
  • Supports immune deficiency treatments
